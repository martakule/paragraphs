Activity: How Do You Communicate?

Effective communication is an essential skill to have in the business world. Employers are interested in hiring individuals who are able to communicate clearly and effectively. In order to improve your communication skills, it’s helpful to know what your communication style is. Take this quiz to find out how you communicate!

In this quiz, you will answer a series of questions that will help you to discover how you communicate. When answering these questions, try to think of how you would answer in a professional environment. There are no wrong answers, so be honest!

Great! You’ve completed the quiz. Before we find out how you communicate, let’s review the four different styles of communication.
The chart below shows two parts to communication style: how direct we are and how thoughtful we are.

Some people can be very direct in expressing what they want or think, while others might simply imply their feelings. Likewise, people can be very thoughtful and consider what other people might want or need, while others may ignore this completely. Most people will find themselves to be a combination of the different styles.

What is your highest score? This score reflects the communication style you use most of the time when in a working environment. It’s also worth noting your next highest score, especially if it’s only a small difference.

If you are an Aggressive communicator, you are direct in expressing needs, wants, and opinions, with little thought for other people’s needs, wants, or opinions.

If you are a Passive Aggressive communicator, you make sure people are aware of your needs, wants, and opinions, but in an indirect way, and with little thought for other people’s needs, wants, or opinions.

If you are a Passive communicator, you don’t express your needs, wants, and opinions, putting other people’s wants, needs, and opinions above your own.

If you are an Assertive communicative, you are clear and direct in expressing needs, wants, and opinions, but you are still respectful of other people’s needs, wants, and opinions.

Debriefing

While there aren’t any right or wrong answers, most people strive to be assertive, especially in the workplace. Your colleagues or clients will be constantly forming opinions about you, based on your tone of voice, body language, or actions. Being aware of your communication style can help you to make sure you are making your voice heard, but in a considerate and respectful way.
