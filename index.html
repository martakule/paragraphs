Activity: Paragraph Pile-Up

Paragraphs are the building blocks of effective writing. Each paragraph presents one main idea. One important piece of an excellent paragraph is coherence. Your sentences should flow from one to the next in a logical manner. There are several techniques that can help you achieve this flow. Make sure to carry over a topic from sentence to sentence. Use pronouns to carry over thoughts throughout the paragraph. Finally, make use of transitional words and phrases to segue between sentences. This activity will help you practice these techniques to create the perfect, coherent paragraph. You can review your text before or after to get more information on the three techniques.

In this activity, you will be given scrambled paragraphs. Try to unscramble them into the correct order to create logical and coherent paragraphs! ( Hint: Try picking out the topic sentence first.)

Paragraph 1

  • The purpose of this report is to update you on the current expansion project.
  • In 2011, we began clinical trials of the new drug, Paropraxolin with patients from St. Mary’s hospital.
  • The results of the clinical trials have been positive, so the company decided last year to expand the trials to hospitals across the country.
  • Working with me on this project are Raj Patel and Dina Laskey, students from Dalston University on a work term funded by a grant from the Anxiety Disorder Association of Canada.
  • The potential market for Paropraxolin remains strong.

Paragraph 2

  • Our goal is to help your students develop essential mathematical skills using the learning methods that suit them best – through supportive individualized workshops, consultations, and hands-on experience.
  • We understand that the self-learning program and personalized training units currently used by your students to address weaknesses in their math skills do not provide them with the level of continuing support and suggestions for improvement both teachers and students are hoping to see.
  • Our proposed training program is designed to ensure your students become more confident and capable in their mathematical skills.
  • These new methods will allow students to build on the strong basic mathematical skills they may already have and to polish, master, and apply those skills to real-life situations they will encounter in their future careers.
  • A more successful program that meets those expectations and delivers the desired learning outcomes involves a combination of several different learning methods, including classroom instruction, regular individual practice, and immediate constructive feedback.

Paragraph 3

  • The characters represent a range of familiar social stereotypes and cultural values – mischievous kids, dislike for school, greedy and cold-hearted boss.
  • In a large industrial nation such as Canada, the media are woven into the complex social and cultural fabric.
  • And the humour is created by playing off our knowledge of these ideas.
  • For instance sitcoms, like The Simpsons, are written to play off our knowledge of and understanding of the world.
  • Similarly, sports, and hockey in particular, are a key element in our culture, and much media content is devoted to them, promoting values familiar to Canadians.
  • Media are central to how we come to understand and share in our culture and society.
  • They employ social institutions we recognize and understand, such as the nuclear family, schools, and corporations.

Let’s try something a little trickier! This time, there will be two main ideas in one paragraph. Can you unscramble them to create two logical, coherent paragraphs?

Note: As you drag and drop sentences, make sure to keep them under the correct paragraph headings. The number of sentences per original paragraph is the same as the number of scrambled sentences.

Paragraph 4

  • Another source of customer dissatisfaction is a lack of flexibility in the service we provide.
  • However, since late 2012 our company has not been equipped to meet the increasing demand for short-run work, such as real estate books and stock prospectuses, which now accounts for 65 per cent of our business.
  • Other print shops have the capability of updating large print runs on demand without having to commit to one version for the entire run.
  • Due to slow turnarounds on short-run rush jobs, many of our long-time customers are reluctantly turning to our competitors for quicker delivery and overnight service.

Paragraph 5

  • Below are two solutions other print shops have implemented to reduce turnaround times and eliminate waste.
  • Revenues for the first and second quarters of 2013 were down by 15 per cent from the same period in 2012, in part due to the loss of accounts for short-run business.
  • Until recently, our offset lithographic environment has been effective in driving volume.
  • When one of our customers inspects advance copies and decides changes are needed, an entire press run, often in excess of 10,000 copies, has to be discarded.

Paragraph 6

  • Audiences, in other words, are not already pre-assembled, waiting for the show to start.
  • Media organizations conduct regular surveys to determine who their audiences are, their age, sex, income bracket, education level, media usage, etc., what their needs are, and to what extent they are satisfied with the services they're receiving.
  • These data form a feedback mechanism by which audience communicate to media organizations through their consumption activities.
  • Audiences need to be built through a number of activities that fall under the general category of marketing.

Paragraph 7

  • Whether or not media organizations are structured as commercial enterprises, great effort is expended in identifying audience, determining their needs and consumption habits, maximizing their numbers, maintaining their loyalty, and, in the case of media that carry advertising, matching audiences to appropriate advertisers.
  • Television and radio stations subscribe to the ratings services, newspapers and magazines subscribe to circulation auditing services, movie companies track box-office receipts, and websites count page views to get a measure of their connection to audiences and users, and who exactly they are connecting with.
  • Marketing includes surveys of actual and potential customers, analysis of ratings, circulation and/ or attendance data, advertising of all kinds, other forms of publicity, and promotional campaigns.

Debriefing

Keep the techniques you’ve practiced in this exercise in mind the next time you are working on a writing task, from the next e-mail you send to the next essay you write.
